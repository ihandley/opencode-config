## About Dandy

Dandy is transforming the massive and antiquated dental industry—an industry worth over $400B. Backed by some of the world's leading venture capital firms, we're on an ambitious mission to simplify and modernize every function of the dental practice through technology. As we expand our reach globally, Dandy is building the operating system for dental offices around the world—empowering clinicians and their teams with technology, innovation, and world-class support to achieve more for their practices, their people, and their patients.

## About the Team

The team is a product engineering team focused on building 0-to-1 products that elevate the clinical experience. From smarter workflows to real-time feedback, we explore new ideas across the care journey that drive outcomes, delight users, and advance Dandy's mission. If you're excited by experimentation, ambiguity, and shaping strategy through execution, this is the team for you.

## What You'll Do

- Define the technical strategy for new products while co-driving product strategy with cross-functional partners
- Lead engineering efforts across the stack, from architecture through implementation and delivery
- Serve as a technical thought partner to product and design, grounding decisions in both vision and pragmatic execution
- Uphold a high bar for engineering quality, reliability, and operational excellence
- Shape and reinforce a team culture focused on speed, experimentation, and continuous learning
- Guide the team through ambiguity and rapid iteration, helping them move fast without sacrificing long-term quality
- Mentor engineers through design reviews, pairing, and strategic planning — raising the bar for the entire group
- Act as a steward for the long-term technical health and direction of the team

- Proven ability to influence team culture and set standards for quality, velocity, and innovation
- Deep fluency with modern web technologies (React, TypeScript, GraphQL, Python, etc.)

- Product-minded and user-obsessed — you think in outcomes, not just outputs
- A bias toward ownership, fast execution, and raising the bar
